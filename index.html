Welcome to the Giant of Africa

Nigeria is a powerhouse of culture, commerce, and creativity. As the most populous nation in Africa with an estimated population of 237 million in 2025, it offers a vibrancy unmatched anywhere else. This handbook is your essential companion, whether you are here to close a deal in Lagos, explore the ancient walls of Kano, or hike the plateaus of Jos.

This guide is endorsed by the Federal Government to ensure you have accurate, official information regarding your rights, responsibilities, and opportunities. It incorporates 2025 updates, including the phasing out of Visa-on-Arrival (effective May 1, 2025) and stricter overstay penalties (from August 1, 2025).

Separation of Powers

At federal and state levels: Executive (President/Governor), Legislature (National/State Assembly), Judiciary (Supreme/High Courts). The 1999 Constitution is supreme; any conflicting law is void.

Fundamental Rights (Chapter IV, 1999 Constitution)

These rights apply to ALL persons in Nigeria, including foreigners. Enforceable in High Courts.

Right Description & Context
Right to Life & Dignity No deprivation of life except by court order. Protected from torture, slavery, inhuman treatment.
Right to Fair Hearing Presumed innocent. Entitled to lawyer, public trial within reasonable time.
Right to Personal Liberty No arbitrary arrest. Informed of reason immediately; court within 24-48 hours.
Freedom of Movement Travel freely within Nigeria with valid papers. Police checkpoints common; carry ID.
Freedom from Discrimination No bias based on ethnicity, sex, religion, origin.
Freedom of Thought & Religion Practice any faith or none; no forced conversion.
Freedom of Expression Hold/express opinions, but not hate speech or sedition.
Right to Private Life Protected from unlawful searches; privacy of home/correspondence.

Enforcement: Contact your Embassy first, then apply to State/Federal High Court. No cost for fundamental rights suits.

Legal System & Enforcement

Blends English common law with local systems. English is official language in courts.

Common Law

Nationwide for criminal/civil matters, contracts, federal offenses (Criminal Code in South).

Sharia Law

In 12 Northern states for Muslims (personal/criminal; Penal Code). Affects alcohol/dress in those areas.

Customary Law

Ethnic traditions for local disputes, chieftaincy, land in communities.

Key Agencies

NPF (Police): Law/order, checkpoints.
EFCC: Financial crimes, fraud (419).
NDLEA: Drugs; zero tolerance.
DSS: National security.
ICPC: Corruption, bribery.
NDPA 2023: Data privacy (GDPR-like).

Visa & Immigration (NIS) - 2025 Updates

All applications via portal.immigration.gov.ng. Visa-on-Arrival phased out May 1, 2025; e-Visa mandatory. Overstay penalties strict from Aug 1, 2025 (fines/deportation/bans). Landing/Exit Cards required from May 1, 2025.

ECOWAS citizens: Visa-free for 90 days (tourism/business only).

CERPAC: The "Green Card" (Digital from Aug 2025)

Mandatory for >56 days stay/work. Apply via new portal cerpac.immigration.gov.ng. Separate ECI insurance $1,000/year.

Fees (Annual, Unchanged):

  • Private Sector: $2,000
  • Students/Missionaries: $400-$600
  • Spouse of Nigerian: Gratis

Requirements:

  • STR Visa, Passport
  • Appointment Letter, Quota Approval
  • Board Resolution, IMM22 Form

Medical Advice (2025)

  • Malaria: Prophylaxis (Malarone/Doxy); nets essential. Endemic year-round.
  • Water/Food: Bottled water only; avoid ice/street food if sensitive.
  • Vaccines: Yellow Fever mandatory; Hep A/B, Typhoid recommended.
  • Hospitals: Lagos: Reddington, Lagoon; Abuja: Nizamiye, National. Evacuation insurance advised.
  • Security: Reconsider travel overall; safe in upscale Lagos/Abuja. Avoid North East.

Cultural Safety

  • Respect religious sites; modest dress in North.
  • Avoid night travel; use apps for rides.
  • Beware scams; use official taxis.

No. F-class visas prohibit employment. Use TWP/STR for work; violations lead to deportation.

From Aug 1, 2025: $50-100/day fines + detention/deportation/ban (0-90 days: $1,000+).

Yes; need passport, CERPAC, BVN (biometric at bank).
